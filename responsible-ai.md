# Responsible AI Statement – CareLens

## Overview

CareLens is a healthcare-focused dashboard designed to transform structured patient data into clear, readable summaries and risk insights. The system uses rule-based logic and optional AI-generated summaries to assist with understanding patient information.

This project is built strictly for **educational and demonstration purposes** using **synthetic patient data**.

---

## Use of Synthetic Data

CareLens only uses **synthetic data generated by Synthea** or simplified demo data.

- No real patient data is used
- No personally identifiable information (PII) is stored
- The system is not connected to any real healthcare system

---

## AI Usage

CareLens includes an optional AI-powered summary feature using a Hugging Face model.

The AI component is used to:
- Generate short, readable summaries of patient data
- Highlight key risk factors in a human-friendly format

The AI does NOT:
- Make diagnoses
- Provide medical advice
- Replace clinical judgment

---

## Limitations

The system has several important limitations:

- Risk scores are based on simplified rule-based logic
- AI summaries may be incomplete or imperfect
- Data parsing from XML/JSON may miss certain fields
- The system does not guarantee medical accuracy

---

## Safety and Responsibility

CareLens is designed with the following safeguards:

- Clear labeling that data is synthetic
- No real-world deployment capability
- Fallback summaries if AI fails
- Defensive programming to prevent crashes on missing data

---

## Intended Use

This project is intended for:

- Demonstration in hackathons
- Educational exploration of healthcare data systems
- Prototyping ideas in clinical data visualization

This project is NOT intended for:

- Clinical decision-making
- Patient diagnosis or treatment
- Use in real healthcare environments

---

## Ethical Considerations

We recognize that healthcare AI systems must be:

- Transparent
- Safe
- Interpretable
- Non-misleading

CareLens reflects these principles by:
- Clearly communicating its limitations
- Avoiding overclaiming AI capabilities
- Keeping outputs simple and explainable

---

## Future Improvements

If extended further, CareLens would require:

- Validation with real clinical experts
- Stronger data privacy controls
- More robust and validated models
- Regulatory compliance (HIPAA, etc.)

---

## Conclusion

CareLens demonstrates how AI and structured data can improve information accessibility, but it must be used responsibly and with full awareness of its limitations.

It is a prototype, not a production medical system.
